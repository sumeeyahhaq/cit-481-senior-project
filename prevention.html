Prevention

This page talks about prevention of COVID-19 spread. It shares with tips and tricks that you can do in order to stop or slow the spread of COVID-19. Follow the guidelines to help protect yourself from contracting and transmitting SARS-CoV-2.

Stay informed with live COVID-19 updates

How to prevent COVID-19

Wear a mask

Please make sure to have the mask cover your nose and month and wear a mask in public to help prevent getting and speading COVID-19. And take off your mask carefully, try not to touch outside surface area of the mask and discard the mask immediately

Stay 6 feet apart and avoid crowds

COVID-19 spreads mainly among people who are in close contact (within about 6 feet) for a prolonged period. Spread happens when an infected person coughs, sneezes, or talks, and droplets from their mouth or nose are launched into the air and land in the mouths or noses of people nearby. The droplets can also be inhaled into the lungs

Wash your hands properly

By following the steps below will provide you with clean hands, Wash your hands at least 20 seconds. You can use Hand Sanitizer (at least conatins 60% alcohol), just follow the same instruction as washing your hands guide and make sure to rub until your hands dry before touch anything else

Clean and disinfect

Clean and disinfect frequently touched surface daily such as tables, doornobs, lightswitch, countertops, toilets, faucets, and sinks

Monitor your health daily

  • Be alert for symptoms
  • Take your temperature
  • Get tested if you have had closed contact to COVID-19 infected person or not sure about your sympthops. Learn more about testing

Get Vaccinated

  • You should get a COVID-19 vaccine when it available to you. Learn more about vaccine

Testing

There are 2 types of testing

1. Viral Testing

  • Throat Swab - a test that is commonly used to diagnose bacterial infection in the throat
  • Nose Swab (PCR test) - widely used to diagnose active severe acute respiratory syndrome coronavirus2

Which one is better?

According to Lansing State Journal mentioned, "The PCR test which nasal swab have a higher sensitivity to detect the coronavirus because it is sensitive enough to detect the smallest traces of the virus." The nose swab is able to collect in deeper area than throat swab, so it allow to detect the virus in deeper area and give more accurate result.

How does the test work?

Both tests using PCR method which is Polymerase Chain Reaction - detects the virus’s RNA in a sample taken from you tested which will replicate enough copies of any viral RNA that present in the sample. This testing involves specially designed “primers” and “probes” which attach themselves to specific sequences of the virus’s genetic code, and send out a signal that indicates the genetic material has been found.

2. Anti-Body Testing

It tells you if you had past infection. An antibody test is a screening for things called antibodies in your blood. Your body makes these when it fights an infection, like COVID-19. The same thing happens when you get a vaccine, like a flu shot. That’s how you build immunity to a virus.

Who should get tested?

  • People who have symptoms of COVID-19.
  • People who have had close contact (within 6 feet for a total of 15 minutes or more) with someone with confirmed COVID-19.
  • People who have taken part in activities that put them at higher risk for COVID-19 because they cannot socially distance as needed, such as travel, attending large social or mass gatherings, or being in crowded indoor settings.
  • People who have been asked or referred to get testing by their healthcare provider, local or state ​health department.
